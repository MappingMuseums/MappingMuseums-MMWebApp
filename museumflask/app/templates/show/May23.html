Decisions / discussions

Years open and years closed data

The two columns covering from and to will be amalgamated into a single column.

On individual museums page: When we have a precise year for opening to the public, then that should be used, if not then a date range (from to) will be shown next to the museum name.

In browse: When we have a precise year for opening to the public, then that should be shown next to the museum name, if not then a date range (from to)

Question: In browse - if a museum opens sometime between 1973 and 1978 would it be shown under the folder that reads 70s or the folder that reads 1973?

In search: There will be a facility for deciding whether users search by the precise date or a fuzzy date. If the former, the search will only pick up entries where we know the exact year, if the later then it will include museums where the date selected falls within the range of possible years open.

Also, to have an additional option where the user provides a date range eg. 1978-1991 (rather than using two filters)

There is an additional column which lists -Fdate known to be open which is when we have no information. For now, this will not be factored into the database. Instead the focus should remain on dates of opening as that allows us to track emergence rather than presence.

Governance data

We do not need two columns where one reads independent and one reads english heritage or National trust. Rather the database should understand that EH and NT are independent and arrange them accordingly.
